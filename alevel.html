ADVANCED LEVEL (A' LEVEL)


Our Advanced level program serves over 240 students from S.5 to S.6. Academics centre around a Uganda National curriculum, including the following subjects:

S101 General Paper

Focuses on general knowledge, current affairs, and critical thinking skills.

S475 Subsidiary Mathematics

Introduction to basic mathematical concepts such as algebra, geometry, and calculus.

S850 Subsidiary Computer

Covers fundamentals of computer science: hardware, software, programming.

Food and Nutrition

Science of food, nutrition, and its relation to health and well-being.

P210 History

Study of historical events and their present and future impacts.

P220 Economics

Economic principles, market structures, and role in society.

P230 Entrepreneurship Education

Basics of starting and managing businesses: marketing, finance, innovation.

P245 Christian Religious Education

Christian teachings, scriptures, and life application.

P250 Geography

Study of the Earth's physical features, environments, and the relationship between people and their surroundings.

P310 Literature in English

Analyzes English literary works, from classical to modern, with a focus on themes and writing styles.

P320 Kiswahili

Study of the Kiswahili language, including grammar, vocabulary, and cultural context.

P364 Runyankore – Rukiga

Introduction to the Runyankore-Rukiga language, covering basic grammar, vocabulary, and cultural aspects.

P425 Mathematics

An advanced study of mathematical concepts, including calculus, algebra, and statistics.

P510 Physics

Introduction to the fundamental laws of physics, including mechanics, thermodynamics, and electricity.

P515 Agriculture

Covers the science and practice of farming, including crop production, animal husbandry, and sustainable agriculture.

P525 Chemistry

Study of the properties, composition, and behavior of matter, including organic and inorganic chemistry.

P530 Biology

Study of living organisms, focusing on structure, function, evolution, and ecology.

P615 Fine Art

Visual arts: painting, sculpture, digital media, creation and analysis.
